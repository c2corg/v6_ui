The global grade offers an overall estimation of the difficulties of an itinerary and how sustained they are, as well as altitude and the normal duration of the itinerary.

WARNING! The grade uses the same letter coding as the global alpine climbing grade but they do not overlap: A snow couloir can easily be graded D for skiing while its mountaineering grade would not exceed PD or AD.
  • F : Easy.
  • PD : Slightly difficult.
  • AD : Somewhat difficult.
  • D : Difficult.
  • TD : Very difficult.
  • ED : Extremely difficult.

The last grade is open: ED- or ED1, ED or ED2, ED+ or ED3, then ED4, ED5, ED6.

See also the help on grades for ski-touring and ski-mountaineering.
